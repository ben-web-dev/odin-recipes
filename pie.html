Cottage Pie

Cottage pie, a British comfort food staple, has a history dating back to the 18th century. Originally devised as a thrifty dish for using up leftovers, it features minced meat, typically beef, cooked with onions, carrots, and peas, topped with creamy mashed potatoes. Its enduring popularity stems from its simplicity and hearty flavors, making it a beloved dish for family gatherings and cozy nights in.

Ingredients

Filling

  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 4 rashers smoked streaky bacon, finely chopped
  • 2 medium onions, finely chopped
  • 2 carrots, trimmed and finely chopped
  • 2 celery sticks, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves finely chopped
  • 2-3 sprigs rosemary leaves picked and finely chopped
  • 500g beef mince

Topping

  • 900g potatoes, peeled and chopped
  • 50g butter
  • 75ml milk
  • 75g Parmesan, grated, plus extra to sprinkle on top

Method

  1. Put a large saucepan on a medium heat and add 1 tbsp olive oil.
  2. Add 4 finely chopped bacon rashers and fry for 10 mins until golden and crisp.
  3. Reduce the heat and add the onions, carrots, celery sticks, garlic cloves, and the rosemary, all finely chopped.
  4. Fry for 10 mins. Stir the veg often until it softens.
  5. Increase the heat to medium-high, add 500g beef mince and cook stirring for 3-4 mins until the meat is browned all over.
  6. Add 2 tins plum tomatoes, the finely chopped leaves from ¾ small pack basil, 1 tsp dried oregano, 2 bay leaves, 2 tbsp tomato purée, 1 beef stock cube, 1 deseeded and finely chopped red chilli (if using), 125ml red wine and 6 halved cherry tomatoes. Stir with a wooden spoon, breaking up the plum tomatoes.
  7. Bring to the boil, reduce to a gentle simmer and cover with a lid. Cook for 1 hr 15 mins stirring occasionally, until you have a rich, thick sauce.
  8. Preheat the oven to 200°C (180°C fan).
  9. Mash the cooked potatoes with butter, milk, and grated Parmesan until smooth. Season to taste.
  10. Spread the cooked meat mixture into a baking dish, then top with the mashed potatoes, spreading evenly.
  11. Sprinkle with extra grated Parmesan.
  12. Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes or until the topping is golden and the filling is bubbling.
